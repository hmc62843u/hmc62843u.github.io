Virtual Marking for Founders

If you have an issued patent and need a simple public patent-marking page, this is the small, practical version. No broad engagement. Just a clean virtual marking surface and founder-readable support.

By Andrew Leung, founder of W&Patent · OpenFor member
Canonical source: https://wpatent.com/virtual-marking.htm

What virtual marking is for

Virtual marking gives a patented product a public URL that associates the product with the issued patent number. If you already have an issued patent and a product in the market, this page can serve as that marking surface.

Who this helps

This is for founders who do not need a broad patent engagement. They need a clean public page, a practical explanation of what belongs on it, and help keeping the surface accurate as products or patent numbers change.

Not every founder needs virtual marking. If the project is still earlier-stage or the patent status is not yet an issued patent tied to a product, a founder project page may be the better public surface. See Dashing Robo for that pattern.

What Andrew helps with

Andrew helps founders set up the page, keep the language straightforward, and make sure the public surface stays easy to maintain. If you need broader judgment about what to protect before you get to this stage, start with the strategy guide.
