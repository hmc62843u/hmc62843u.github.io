# Trust Chain Template Design

Date: 2026-05-07
Project: Free authority-template and scorecard system for founder-led B2B firms
Status: Draft for review

## Summary

W&Patent will publish a free authority-page template that helps founder-led B2B firms turn a website into a clearer, more verifiable authority surface for AI search. The template is not a ranking hack and does not promise recommendation outcomes. Its role is to improve structural clarity, authorship visibility, proof presentation, and machine-readable trust signals so a company is easier for humans and AI systems to discover, interpret, and verify.

The strategic model is Andrew-led and W&Patent-anchored:

- Andrew Leung is the primary trust anchor
- W&Patent is the canonical entity and operating platform
- the free template is the public methodology artifact
- the Trust Chain Scorecard is the first conversion offer

The system is designed for unknown but legitimate companies with real expertise, low brand recognition, and a need for a stronger chain of trust across domain, author, and content.

## Core Thesis

Unknown companies often publish websites that describe what they do but do not make it easy to verify:

- who the entity is
- who the expert is
- what the company believes
- what proof supports the claims
- how the page can be parsed by machines

The free template exists to address that gap. It gives a company a high-leverage starting point for publishing one strong authority page built around a visible trust chain:

- Domain
- Author
- Content

This is a trust-architecture starter, not a guarantee of SEO, GEO, or AEO outcomes.

## Goals

- Publish a free template that acts as both a useful resource and a visible authority artifact
- Strengthen Andrew Leung's personal authority in a way that transfers cleanly to W&Patent
- Establish W&Patent as the canonical home of the Trust Chain methodology
- Create a clear bridge from free public asset to a paid or consultative service
- Make the initial offer broadly useful for founder-led B2B firms rather than only patent-heavy businesses
- Provide a structure that improves extractability, readability, and verifiability for AI-oriented crawlers and retrieval systems

## Non-Goals

- No promise that template adoption will cause ranking gains, citations, or recommendation by AI systems
- No attempt to solve an entire site architecture in version one
- No requirement that the first version support every company type or every business model
- No attempt to present W&Patent as a fully live SaaS platform through this template
- No heavy gating of the main free asset

## Audience

### Primary Audience

Founder-led B2B firms with low brand recognition and real expertise to demonstrate.

These companies usually:

- have a custom domain or plan to move toward one
- have a founder or expert who can credibly represent the method
- have services, software, or expertise that benefit from trust and explanation
- need a stronger narrative and structural layer than a generic brochure site provides

### Secondary Audience

- solo consultants evolving toward a more structured authority presence
- small expert-led teams publishing a framework or point of view
- early-stage companies with real substance but weak public trust packaging

### Not For

- teams looking for instant ranking wins from installing a template
- companies with no real expert, method, or proof behind the claims
- businesses that want only gated lead capture and no public authority assets
- teams that do not intend to maintain a canonical web presence over time

## Positioning Model

The recommended positioning is:

**Andrew-led, W&Patent-anchored methodology bridge**

This means:

- Andrew Leung is presented as the expert and originator of the framework
- W&Patent is presented as the canonical entity where the method is explained and commercialized
- the template is a practical implementation artifact, not a detached side project
- the scorecard is a W&Patent service led by Andrew

This approach is preferred over a pure personal-brand play because it transfers trust to the company. It is preferred over a pure company-first play because Andrew is currently the strongest trust signal.

## Value Proposition

Primary statement:

**A free template for founder-led startups and B2B firms to turn a website into a verifiable authority surface for AI search.**

Expanded statement:

**Build a clearer chain of trust across your domain, founder, and content with structured pages, explicit entities, and machine-readable trust signals that are easier for humans and AI systems to discover, interpret, and verify.**

Honesty boundary:

**This template helps structure trust. It does not manufacture it.**

## Asset System

The first version should use four connected assets with distinct jobs.

### 1. GitHub Repo

Job:

- prove Andrew builds real, reusable assets
- act as a public artifact with timestamps, commits, stars, and forks
- provide the free template and documentation

### 2. W&Patent Landing Page

Job:

- act as the canonical home of the methodology
- explain the Trust Chain framework in plain language
- connect Andrew, W&Patent, and the template into one narrative

### 3. Template Demo Page

Job:

- show the trust architecture in action
- make the template feel concrete and inspectable
- support both human evaluation and machine-readability claims

### 4. Trust Chain Scorecard Offer

Job:

- convert qualified interest into an Andrew-led W&Patent service engagement
- turn the free resource into a commercial conversation without hard-selling the template

The intended asset hierarchy is:

- framework creates authority
- template creates utility
- scorecard creates conversion

## Narrative Architecture

The authority page should not lead with a download pitch. It should lead with a point of view:

**Unknown companies do not need more vague content. They need a clearer chain of trust.**

Recommended narrative flow:

1. Hero
2. Method centerpiece
3. Template as implementation
4. Proof and verification
5. Audit / scorecard bridge

The method centerpiece is the most important section because it makes the page memorable and citable. The free template is the mechanism for applying the method, not the entire value by itself.

## Messaging Strategy

The messaging should make this idea feel obvious:

**This template helps unknown but legitimate companies become easier to verify.**

### Top-Level Promise

Free template for building a verifiable authority surface for AI search.

### Problem Statement

Most startup websites describe what they do, but they do not make it easy for humans or AI systems to verify who they are, who is behind them, and why they should be trusted.

### Framework Statement

Trust is easier to build when the chain is explicit:

- Domain
- Author
- Content

### What The Template Does

- clarifies the company entity
- makes expert authorship visible
- surfaces proof and citations
- exposes machine-readable trust signals
- gives a company a stable authority-page structure

### What The Template Does Not Promise

- guaranteed rankings
- guaranteed recommendation by answer engines
- guaranteed citation or reuse

The messaging should stay practical, strategic, and slightly opinionated. It should avoid both "SEO trick" language and abstract manifesto language.

## Template Content Model

Version one should help a company publish one strong authority page, not an entire site system.

### Required Sections

1. Hero
   - what the company is
   - who it serves
   - the main claim in plain language

2. Founder / Expert Identity
   - visible person
   - role
   - expertise
   - relationship to the company

3. Method / Point of View
   - framework, process, or thesis
   - the key reason the company is worth trusting

4. Proof
   - credentials
   - public artifacts
   - case references
   - citations
   - linked resources

5. Structured FAQ / Explanation
   - short trust questions and answers
   - retrieval-friendly formatting

6. Machine-Readable Layer
   - JSON-LD
   - explicit organization/person relationships
   - stable URLs
   - lightweight manifest or endpoint when relevant

7. Primary CTA
   - request a Trust Chain Scorecard
   - or a closely related authority audit action

## Minimum Trust Surface

The template should cover these five trust elements:

1. Founder / expert block
2. Entity / company block
3. Method / framework block
4. Proof / verification block
5. Machine-readable block

The method / framework block should be the centerpiece. That is what turns the page from a generic business page into an authority artifact.

## Trust Chain Scorecard Offer

The first service offer should be:

**Trust Chain Scorecard**

Recommended form:

- async scorecard
- for founder-led B2B firms
- focused first on trust-chain quality rather than general conversion optimization
- public with permission when the client approves

Why this offer:

- more scalable than a live consult
- clearer and more repeatable than a recorded teardown for version one
- easier to reuse as case-study proof when permission is granted
- tightly aligned to the template's core promise

The scorecard should grade the trust chain first:

- domain clarity
- author clarity
- company-entity clarity
- method articulation
- proof quality
- citation quality
- structure and machine-readability

## Cross-Linking Rules

Trust transfer depends on explicit linkage across assets.

Every major asset should reinforce the same identity chain:

**Andrew Leung -> founder of W&Patent -> W&Patent methodology -> Trust Chain Template -> Trust Chain Scorecard**

Required linkage rules:

- the GitHub repo links back to W&Patent
- the W&Patent page links to the GitHub repo and demo
- Andrew is consistently described as "Andrew Leung, founder of W&Patent"
- the demo page points to both the methodology page and the scorecard offer
- supporting posts and bios reuse the same framing

## Strategic Risks And Guardrails

### Risk 1: Overclaiming

If the template implies that AI agents will recommend a company because of page structure alone, credibility drops quickly.

Guardrail:

- present the template as a trust-architecture starter, not a recommendation engine

### Risk 2: Empty Structure

A well-structured page with weak underlying evidence still feels thin.

Guardrail:

- explicitly state that the template structures trust but does not create it from nothing

### Risk 3: Split Identity

If Andrew's authority grows without clear transfer to W&Patent, the company does not benefit fully.

Guardrail:

- keep the founder-to-company linkage consistent everywhere

### Risk 4: Too Broad Too Early

If the template tries to serve every company type, it will become generic.

Guardrail:

- keep version one focused on founder-led B2B firms with real expertise and low brand recognition

### Risk 5: Product Confusion

If users cannot tell whether this is a framework, a template, a service, or a SaaS product, the story becomes muddy.

Guardrail:

- framework equals idea
- template equals free implementation
- scorecard equals service
- W&Patent equals canonical platform

## Success Conditions

This project is successful at the design level if:

- the free template is useful even without buying anything
- the method is memorable enough to be repeated and cited
- the relationship between Andrew and W&Patent feels coherent
- the scorecard offer feels like a natural next step rather than a bait-and-switch
- the positioning is honest about what structure can and cannot do
