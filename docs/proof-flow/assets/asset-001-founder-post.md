## Draft

Most startup patent advice gets reduced to "file early" or "build a portfolio." That framing skips the harder question: what part of the business is actually worth protecting because it changes leverage?

In one illustrative early-stage B2B scenario I wrote up for W&Patent, the visible dashboard was not the main strategic asset. The stronger protection target was the workflow logic that made onboarding faster, harder to copy, and easier to defend in diligence, partnership, and commercialization conversations.

That is the difference between patent activity and patent strategy. Filing broadly around visible features can create motion without improving bargaining position. Protecting the mechanism that changes copy risk, buyer confidence, or future deal leverage is usually the stronger move.
