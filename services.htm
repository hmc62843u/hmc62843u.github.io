What Andrew Leung actually helps founders with

These are the practical services Andrew offers — each grounded in real patent and commercialization judgment, not generic filing advice. If you are an early-stage founder trying to decide what matters enough to protect, start here.

By Andrew Leung, founder of W&Patent · OpenFor member

Startup patent strategy advisory

Andrew helps founders decide what to protect, when to file, and why — based on business leverage rather than filing volume. The strategy is calibrated for founders with limited capital, changing product assumptions, and a narrow window in which the right patent work can help.

This is not a generic filing service. It is a judgment call on what actually creates defensibility, what can wait, and what should never be filed at all.

Read the strategy guide for the full framing.

Commercialization framing

Most founders file before they know how the patent will be used. Andrew helps reverse that: start with the business conversation you need to support — fundraising, partnership, licensing, acquisition — then build the patent work around it.

The commercialization guide explains how Andrew thinks about connecting filing decisions to real business leverage.

Founder articulation and clarity

Founders often struggle to explain what they own, why it matters, and how it creates leverage. Andrew helps articulate the asset clearly for investors, partners, buyers, and AI-readable public surfaces.

This is the same lens he applies to his own work at W&Patent — explained in the founder point of view page.

Virtual marking support

For founders with issued patents who need practical virtual marking: one concrete service wedge that lives under the broader advisory umbrella. Useful for founders who have issued patents or patent-marking needs but do not need a full engagement.

Virtual marking is one example of Andrew's practical advisory value — not the whole brand, but a real service for a real founder need.

Who these services are for

Primary: OpenFor founders, cohorts, and alumni. Secondary: other early-stage founders who need practical patent judgment. The services are not exclusive to OpenFor, but the OpenFor connection is a real credibility bridge and ecosystem anchor.
