Who Andrew is

Andrew Leung is a registered patent agent who became an entrepreneur. He has spent more than a decade helping founders use patent strategy to build defensibility, sharper positioning, and long-term commercial leverage. He is also an OpenFor member, which shapes how he thinks about founder needs, limited capital, and practical judgment.

What W&Patent is

W&Patent is Andrew's public site where this view, these services, and the proof live. It is a founder patent strategy and commercialization advisory surface — not a law firm, not a marketplace, not a generic blog. It exists to help founders articulate, protect, and commercialize what actually matters.

What Andrew helps founders with

He helps founders decide what matters enough to protect, connect patent work to commercialization and business leverage, and articulate the asset clearly for the outside world. His approach is practical, high-judgment, and calibrated for founders with limited capital and a narrow window. View services.

How Andrew thinks about patent strategy

Andrew treats patent strategy as a business decision system, not a filing sequence. The question is not whether to file — it is what the patent work should protect or enable in the business. That lens shapes everything on this site. Read the strategy guide for the full framing.

Trust Chain methodology

Andrew created the Trust Chain method at W&Patent. It is a founder-led framework for connecting company identity, founder authority, and proof on the public web — making the business easier to understand, trust, and cite. Explore the methodology.

Commercialization framing

Andrew frames patent commercialization around the conversations that matter — fundraising, partnership, licensing, acquisition. The commercialization guide shows how that logic works in founder-facing terms.
