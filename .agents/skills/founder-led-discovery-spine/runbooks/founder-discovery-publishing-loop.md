# Founder Discovery Publishing Loop

Use this runbook when an agent needs to turn internal measurement into a public founder-facing asset such as a founder post, case note, intro note, teardown, or topic-page seed.

## Purpose

Give agents a disciplined loop for moving from internal evidence to public publishing without dumping raw scorecards into public view or skipping the founder's judgment layer.

## Core Loop

`Measure -> Distill -> Assert -> Publish -> Learn`

## Step 1: Measure

Start with the private evidence layer:

- scorecards
- prompt-run logs
- roadmap notes
- proof-flow feedback
- page-level or domain-level observations

The goal is to answer:

- what is absent
- what is misunderstood
- what is weakly attributed
- what did not improve after a change
- what response signal the founder is now seeing

## Step 2: Distill

Turn the evidence into one clear lesson.

Good distilled insights sound like:

- copy cleanup alone did not change discovery
- founders responded to leverage framing more than filing framing
- the site is understandable once in context but still absent off-site

Bad distilled insights sound like:

- our score moved from 42 to 47
- here is the whole CSV
- the benchmark has twelve rows of differences

One public asset should usually carry one main distilled insight.

## Step 3: Assert

Translate the insight into a founder point of view.

This is the judgment layer.
It is where the founder decides what the evidence means.

Examples:

- patent strategy should protect leverage, not just generate filings
- citation surfaces matter more than homepage polish
- a website can organize authority, but proof beyond the site is what helps it hold up

Do not publish measurement without a point of view.

## Step 4: Publish

Choose the right public format for the assertion:

- `founder post` when the goal is reach and fast signal
- `case note` when the goal is proof and inspectability
- `intro note` when the goal is warm replies and sharper questions
- `community post` when the goal is topic testing inside a known network
- `topic page` when the goal is durable citation surface expansion

Rules:

- publish the distilled insight, not the raw internal machinery
- keep the founder visibly attached to the claim
- link back to the relevant page or proof asset when useful
- prefer one strong idea over a blended mini-report

## Step 5: Learn

After publishing, capture what the market did with the asset:

- replies
- reposts or shares
- click behavior
- follow-up questions
- citation movement later
- whether the wrong part of the message got attention

Then feed that back into the next round of measurement.

## Inputs

- scorecards and prompt-run evidence
- discovery roadmap
- proof-network notes
- proof-flow workspace state
- existing public assets
- founder response or market response

## Outputs

- one distilled insight
- one founder point of view
- one selected publication format
- one public draft or public asset
- one follow-up learning question

## Boundaries

Do not:

- publish raw scorecards directly unless the user explicitly wants a public scorecard
- confuse internal benchmark detail with public proof
- collapse multiple insights into one vague post
- skip the founder point of view layer
- treat public reaction as the only measurement layer

## Practical Heuristic

Use scorecards and internal measurement as the private evidence layer.
Distill one clear insight from that evidence.
Translate it into a founder point of view.
Publish the idea in the smallest public form that can carry it well.
Then learn from the response and feed that back into the next measurement cycle.

## Short Definition

This runbook helps agents turn internal discovery measurement into public founder-facing assets without losing the evidence trail or the founder's judgment.
