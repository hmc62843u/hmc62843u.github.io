Provisional vs NDA: Why the $60 Filing Beats the NDA Every Time

Part of the startup patent strategy series. Start with the pillar guide if you haven't read it yet.

By Andrew Leung, founder of W&Patent
Canonical source: https://wpatent.com/provisional-vs-nda.htm

NDAs won't save you. A provisional will.

Founders obsess over NDAs. They spend hours drafting them, negotiating them, worrying about who signed and who didn't.

Meanwhile, the real tool for protecting your priority date costs $60 and takes an afternoon to file.

You do not need an NDA to talk to investors. You need a provisional patent. Then you can talk to anyone.

VCs generally do not sign NDAs

VC firms do not sign NDAs for pitch meetings. It is not personal — they see hundreds of deals and cannot negotiate confidentiality with every founder. Asking signals inexperience.

Ask Likely response
"Will you sign my NDA before I pitch?" "We don't sign NDAs for meetings."
"Can you at least keep this confidential?" "We operate with discretion, but we can't sign."
"What if I share technical details?" "Then you've potentially disclosed without protection."

The founder who understands this does not fight the NDA battle. They file the provisional first and walk in without asking permission.

The US grace period is real — but it is not a strategy

US law gives you 12 months from public disclosure to file a patent application. So yes — you can pitch without an NDA, then file within a year.

Andrew's view: that does not make it a good idea.

Reality What it means
You can file up to 12 months after disclosure Your US rights survive, but with risk
You must prove the disclosure did not enable prior art Expensive litigation risk later
Your foreign rights are already dead You lost Europe, China, Japan on day one

Relying on the grace period works until it does not. Most founders who lose on this lose foreign rights, not US rights.

Disclosure before filing kills foreign rights permanently

Europe has no grace period. Neither does China. Neither does Japan. Neither do most developed markets.

Country Grace period Pitch before filing?
United States 12 months Risky but possible
Europe None Kills rights permanently
China None Kills rights permanently
Japan None Kills rights permanently

That investor pitch you gave without an NDA? If you had not filed yet, you just lost patent protection for your entire European and Asian market strategy. Permanently.

What an NDA actually does vs what a provisional does

Tool Cost What it protects What it does not
NDA Time (drafting + negotiation) A contractual obligation to stay quiet Foreign rights, copycats, independent invention
Provisional patent $60–$120 Priority date worldwide for 12 months Nothing — it is a placeholder
Both together $60 + a conversation Full optionality —

The NDA creates a legal obligation to keep quiet. The provisional creates a legal right to claim priority. One is a restriction. The other is an asset.

File the provisional. Then pitch freely.

Here is the workflow that costs less than dinner for two:

When Action Cost
Friday morning Write a 10-page description of what you built. Sketch diagrams. Name what's novel. Your time (2 hours)
Friday afternoon File a provisional USPTO application via the electronic filing system $60 (micro entity)
Monday morning Pitch to investors without NDAs. Talk freely. Answer detailed questions. Nothing — you are protected

The provisional does not stop copycats. It does not let you sue. But it plants a flag that says: I possessed this on that date. No NDA can do that.

The $60 solution to a $100k problem

Scenario Outcome
Pitch without NDA, no filing Foreign rights lost. US rights at risk.
Pitch with NDA Rare — most will not sign.
File provisional ($60), then pitch Full optionality. Pitch freely. Foreign rights preserved.

The cheapest solution is also the most effective one. Stop negotiating NDAs. Start filing.

Provisional is not paranoia. It is optionality.

Founders who file provisionals are not hiding their idea. They are buying the freedom to talk openly.

An NDA says: "I don't trust you." A provisional says: "I trust myself. I have already done the work. Let's talk."

One closes doors. The other opens them.
