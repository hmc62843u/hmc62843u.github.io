FAQ

Questions about Andrew Leung, W&Patent, and founder patent strategy.

Who is Andrew Leung?

Andrew Leung is a registered patent agent turned entrepreneur and an OpenFor member. He founded W&Patent to help founders articulate, protect, and commercialize what actually matters. Read more.

What is W&Patent?

W&Patent is Andrew Leung's founder-facing advisory surface — not a law firm, not a marketplace, not a generic blog. It provides practical patent strategy and commercialization judgment for early-stage founders.

Who is W&Patent for?

Primary: OpenFor founders, cohorts, and alumni. Secondary: other early-stage founders who need practical patent judgment. The site is not exclusive to OpenFor — OpenFor functions as a credibility bridge and ecosystem anchor.

What does Andrew help founders with?

Andrew helps founders decide what matters enough to protect, connect patent work to commercialization and business leverage, and articulate the asset clearly for investors, partners, buyers, and AI-readable public surfaces. View services.

What is virtual marking?

Virtual marking is a practical service for founders with issued patents who need to meet patent-marking requirements without physical product labels. It is one concrete example of Andrew's advisory value, not the whole brand. Read about services.

Is Andrew connected to OpenFor?

Yes. Andrew is an OpenFor member. This is a real relationship — not a partnership inflation or ambiguous affiliate label. It shapes how Andrew thinks about founder needs, limited capital, and practical judgment.

How does W&Patent think about startup patent strategy?

W&Patent treats patent strategy as a business decision system, not a filing sequence. The question is what the patent work should protect or enable — leverage, positioning, commercialization — not how many filings to generate. Read the strategy guide.

Should I file a provisional patent?

Often yes when speed matters and you need an early filing date while refining claim strategy. But the real question is what you are protecting and whether it creates leverage for the next stage of your business.

How do I contact Andrew?

Email wp@wpatent.com directly. Andrew reviews inquiries personally.
